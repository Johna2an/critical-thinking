# Critical-thinking book library (index)

One teaching file per book: the book's actual thinking tools, taught in plain words so
you can apply them. Load a single file only when a task wants that book's depth. The
always-relevant operating layer is `SKILL.md` plus the four tool libraries one level up
(`biases-and-judgment.md`, `argument-and-evidence.md`, `forecasting-and-decisions.md`,
`systems-science-and-creativity.md`).

## Bias and judgment
- `thinking-fast-and-slow.md`: Daniel Kahneman. The two-system model and the major biases (anchoring, availability, representativeness, base-rate neglect, the conjunction fallacy, regression to the mean, framing, loss aversion).
- `scout-mindset.md`: Julia Galef. Accuracy-motivated reasoning, the scout-versus-soldier stance, and the thought-experiment self-tests.
- `judgment-managerial-decision-making.md`: Bazerman and Moore. The bias catalog for organizational decisions, the two-system audit, and the rational decision scaffold.
- `rational-choice-uncertain-world.md`: Hastie and Dawes. Decision science, probability, expected utility, and why simple models beat intuition.
- `thinking-in-bets.md`: Annie Duke. Decisions as bets, separating decision quality from outcome quality, and the truthseeking pod.
- `factfulness.md`: Hans Rosling. The ten instincts that distort the world picture and the data habits that correct them.

## Argument and evidence
- `asking-the-right-questions.md`: Browne and Keeley. The full question checklist for analyzing any argument.
- `rulebook-for-arguments.md`: Anthony Weston. Clean argument structure, the authority test, valid forms, and reckoning with objections.
- `thinking-from-a-to-z.md`: Nigel Warburton. Fallacies, the enthymeme, validity versus soundness, and the conditional forms.
- `how-to-read-a-book.md`: Adler and Van Doren. Active and analytical reading, the four questions, and the grounds for valid disagreement.
- `intuition-pumps.md`: Daniel Dennett. Thought-experiment tools (turn the knobs, jootsing) and the bad-tool detectors (the "surely" alarm, rathering, deepities).

## Statistics and bullshit
- `how-to-lie-with-statistics.md`: Darrell Huff. The classic manipulations (the gee-whiz graph, the semi-attached figure, the shifting base) and the five questions that defuse them.
- `calling-bullshit.md`: Bergstrom and West. Black-box reasoning, selection bias, the unsupported denominator, deceptive visualizations, and Brandolini's principle.

## Forecasting and prediction
- `superforecasting.md`: Tetlock and Gardner. Outside-then-inside view, calibration, Brier scoring, Fermi-ization, and small Bayesian updates.
- `signal-and-the-noise.md`: Nate Silver. Bayesian thinking, signal versus noise, overfitting, fox over hedgehog, and risk versus uncertainty.

## Science and epistemics
- `demon-haunted-world.md`: Carl Sagan. The Baloney Detection Kit, falsifiability, proportional evidence, and the default to the prosaic.
- `nonsense-on-stilts.md`: Massimo Pigliucci. The science-pseudoscience demarcation gradient, the expertise triage, and the manufacture of doubt.
- `beginning-of-infinity.md`: David Deutsch. Good explanations are hard to vary, conjecture and criticism, fallibilism, and reach.

## Systems and creativity
- `thinking-in-systems.md`: Donella Meadows. Stocks, flows, feedback loops, delays, the twelve leverage points, and the system traps.
- `lateral-thinking.md`: Edward de Bono. Escaping the dominant pattern through quota, reversal, random stimulation, and suspended judgment.
- `art-of-doing-science-and-engineering.md`: Richard Hamming. Problem selection, back-of-envelope modeling, fundamentals over facts, and how breakthroughs actually happen.
