## Should you take the $400k contract? Lean NO as framed, but negotiate it into a YES.

### Assumptions (state them, because they drive the answer)
- 6 people, 9 months runway implies a burn of roughly $55-75k/month (call it $65k, ~$585k/year all-in). The $400k therefore buys roughly 6 months of runway, not the implied "windfall." After 5 months of effort consumed, the net runway extension is closer to 1-2 months of slack, not a transformation.
- "Entire team for 5 months" means zero core-product progress for over half your remaining runway.
- You are pre-product-market-fit (otherwise you would not be weighing a custom build this heavily).

### Make the invisible cost visible (opportunity-cost lens)
The scarce resource is not money, it is **6 person-months x 5 = 30 person-months of your only engineering capacity at the most fragile stage of the company.** The true cost of the contract is $400k *minus* the value of what those 30 person-months would have produced on the core roadmap. If those months are when you would have hit PMF, shipped the feature that unlocks your real market, or raised your next round on product traction, the opportunity cost can easily exceed $400k.

"Compared to what?" test on the headline benefit: "$400k and longer runway" compared to what? Compared to 5 months of roadmap progress that compounds into fundraising leverage and a defensible product. Cash that buys time you spend building the wrong thing is negative-value time.

### And then what? (second-order lens)
- **First order:** cash in, runway up, team busy. Looks great.
- **Second order:** roadmap freezes for 5 months. Your real customers and competitors do not freeze. You exit the contract 5 months behind, with a codebase carrying a custom feature only one customer wants (ongoing maintenance debt, support burden, distorted architecture). Your best engineers spent half a year on someone else's spec, and morale/retention risk rises if this is not the mission they joined for.
- **Third order:** when the contract ends you are at ~T+5 months with maybe 3-4 months runway and *no new product progress to raise on.* You may have to either chase another custom contract (becoming a services shop, not a product company) or raise from weakness. The "cobra farming" trap: the cash incentive quietly converts you from a venture-scale product into a low-margin custom-dev agency.

### Where the NO flips to YES
Take it if you can restructure so the work is **not** pure opportunity-cost destruction:
1. **Overlap with the roadmap.** If even 40-50% of the build is reusable platform/core work, the effective cost collapses and this becomes a paid acceleration of your own product. This is the single highest-leverage question to answer.
2. **Carve out the core.** Don't freeze 6/6 people. Ring-fence 1-2 engineers on the core roadmap so product momentum never hits zero; deliver the contract with 4. Slower delivery, but you preserve the compounding asset.
3. **Reprice for the real cost.** $400k for 30 person-months is roughly $13k/person-month, cheap if it also torches your roadmap. Push for $550-650k, milestone-based, plus IP retention so anything reusable stays yours.
4. **Anti-lock-in terms:** you keep IP, no exclusivity, ability to productize the feature for other customers later. That converts a one-off into a possible market signal.

### What would change my mind
- Strong YES if the customer is a credible design partner whose "custom" need is actually validation of your next core feature (then it is funded roadmap, not a detour), or if you are otherwise weeks from dying (a bird in hand beats insolvency).
- Hard NO if it is a bespoke one-off, non-reusable, with IP assignment, taking 100% of the team.

### Sunk-cost check
There is no prior investment here biasing you, so decide purely on forward value. The pull of "but it's $400k" is the trap; weigh it against the forgone roadmap, not against zero.

### Verdict
Do not take it **as offered** (whole team, 5 months, custom-only, $400k). Counter with: ring-fence 1-2 engineers on core, demand reusability/IP, reprice toward ~$600k milestone-based. If they accept and >~40% of the work feeds your roadmap, take it. If they insist on 100% team capture of a non-reusable, IP-assigned build, walk, and instead spend that runway proving PMF, which is the asset that actually keeps you alive.
