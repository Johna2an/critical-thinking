## (a) Piano tuners in Chicago — Fermi estimate

**Decomposition:** Tuners = (pianos needing service ÷ tunings one tuner performs per year), with pianos = population × households × ownership rate.

| Factor | Estimate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Chicago population (city) | ~2.7M | known |
| People per household | ~2.5 | US average |
| Households | ~1.1M | derived |
| Piano ownership rate | ~3% (1 in 30) | pianos far rarer than mid-20th c.; bounds 1-5% |
| Household pianos | ~33,000 | |
| + institutional (schools, churches, studios, bars) | +20% | tuned more often |
| Total pianos serviced | ~40,000 | |
| Effective tunings/piano/year | ~0.75 | many owners neglect tuning |
| Annual tunings demanded | ~30,000 | |
| Tunings per full-time tuner/year | ~900 | ~4/day × 5 days × 45 wks |

**Result:** 30,000 ÷ 900 ≈ **33 tuners.**

The classic textbook answer lands near 50-100 because it assumed much higher piano ownership (1 in 10-20, when pianos were household-common). Modern ownership is genuinely lower, pulling the estimate down. Order of magnitude (dozens) cross-checks against historical directory/BLS-style counts of working tuners in Chicago.

**Verdict (a): ~40 full-time-equivalent piano tuners. 80% confidence range ≈ 20-100.** The robust claim is the order of magnitude: dozens, not thousands and not single digits.

## (b) P(random Fortune 500 CEO has undergrad engineering/hard-science degree vs business/economics)

**Base rate (from repeated S&P/Fortune 500 education studies):**
- Undergrad **engineering**: consistently the single most common major among big-company CEOs, low-to-mid 30s percent (~32%).
- Other **hard sciences** (physics, chem, math, CS, bio): adds ~5-10 pts (~7%).
- → **Engineering or hard science ≈ 38-40%.**
- Business/economics is a comparably large bucket (~30-40%); the rest hold liberal-arts/law-track/other degrees, so the two named camps don't cover everyone.

**Calibration:** Definitions vary (STEM vs engineering-only; "hard science" excludes quantitative economics), and the Fortune 500's tilt toward industrial/tech/energy/manufacturing supports the upper end. Equivalent-bet check: I'd bet at a bit under even odds, which fits ~38%.

**Verdict (b): ~38%, with an 80% confidence interval of roughly 30-45%.** In absolute terms it's a minority (closer to 2-in-5 than a coin flip), but engineering alone is the largest single undergrad field among these CEOs, which is the load-bearing fact.

**Key uncertainties / flags:** (a) hinges entirely on the piano-ownership rate, the softest factor (a 2× error there swings the count 2×); (b) hinges on how "hard science" is delimited and on study sampling (S&P 500 vs Fortune 500, undergrad-only vs highest-degree). Both numbers are order-of-magnitude / base-rate estimates, not lookups.
