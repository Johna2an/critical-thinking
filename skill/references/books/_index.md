# Critical-thinking book library (index)

One teaching file per book: the book's actual thinking tools, taught in plain words so
you can apply them. 51 files in all. Load a single file only when a task wants that
book's depth. The always-relevant operating layer is `SKILL.md`; the nine tool libraries
one level up hold the cross-book synthesis and load by task (`biases-and-judgment.md`,
`argument-and-evidence.md`, `forecasting-and-decisions.md`, `systems-science-and-creativity.md`,
`creativity-and-ideation.md`, `systems-and-complexity.md`, `uncertainty-and-risk.md`,
`models-and-formal-thinking.md`, `epistemology-and-science.md`).

## Bias and judgment
- `thinking-fast-and-slow.md`: Daniel Kahneman. The two-system model and the major biases (anchoring, availability, representativeness, base-rate neglect, the conjunction fallacy, regression to the mean, framing, loss aversion).
- `scout-mindset.md`: Julia Galef. Accuracy-motivated reasoning, the scout-versus-soldier stance, and the thought-experiment self-tests.
- `judgment-managerial-decision-making.md`: Bazerman and Moore. The bias catalog for organizational decisions, the two-system audit, and the rational decision scaffold.
- `rational-choice-uncertain-world.md`: Hastie and Dawes. Decision science, probability, expected utility, and why simple models beat intuition.
- `thinking-in-bets.md`: Annie Duke. Decisions as bets, separating decision quality from outcome quality, and the truthseeking pod.
- `factfulness.md`: Hans Rosling. The ten instincts that distort the world picture and the data habits that correct them.

## Argument and evidence
- `asking-the-right-questions.md`: Browne and Keeley. The full question checklist for analyzing any argument.
- `rulebook-for-arguments.md`: Anthony Weston. Clean argument structure, the authority test, valid forms, and reckoning with objections.
- `thinking-from-a-to-z.md`: Nigel Warburton. Fallacies, the enthymeme, validity versus soundness, and the conditional forms.
- `how-to-read-a-book.md`: Adler and Van Doren. Active and analytical reading, the four questions, and the grounds for valid disagreement.
- `intuition-pumps.md`: Daniel Dennett. Thought-experiment tools (turn the knobs, jootsing) and the bad-tool detectors (the "surely" alarm, rathering, deepities).

## Statistics and bullshit
- `how-to-lie-with-statistics.md`: Darrell Huff. The classic manipulations (the gee-whiz graph, the semi-attached figure, the shifting base) and the five questions that defuse them.
- `calling-bullshit.md`: Bergstrom and West. Black-box reasoning, selection bias, the unsupported denominator, deceptive visualizations, and Brandolini's principle.

## Forecasting and prediction
- `superforecasting.md`: Tetlock and Gardner. Outside-then-inside view, calibration, Brier scoring, Fermi-ization, and small Bayesian updates.
- `signal-and-the-noise.md`: Nate Silver. Bayesian thinking, signal versus noise, overfitting, fox over hedgehog, and risk versus uncertainty.

## Science and epistemics
- `demon-haunted-world.md`: Carl Sagan. The Baloney Detection Kit, falsifiability, proportional evidence, and the default to the prosaic.
- `nonsense-on-stilts.md`: Massimo Pigliucci. The science-pseudoscience demarcation gradient, the expertise triage, and the manufacture of doubt.
- `beginning-of-infinity.md`: David Deutsch. Good explanations are hard to vary, conjecture and criticism, fallibilism, and reach.

## Philosophy of science (V2)
- `structure-scientific-revolutions.md`: Thomas Kuhn. Paradigms, normal science, anomaly and crisis, revolutions, and incommensurability.
- `logic-scientific-discovery.md`: Karl Popper. Falsifiability and demarcation, conjecture and refutation, potential falsifiers, and ad hoc immunization.
- `against-method.md`: Paul Feyerabend. Epistemological anarchism, "anything goes" as history, counterinduction, proliferation, and theory-ladenness.
- `what-is-knowledge.md`: Duncan Pritchard. Justified true belief, the Gettier case, epistemic and veritic luck, sensitivity and safety, and contextual standards.

## Systems and creativity
- `thinking-in-systems.md`: Donella Meadows. Stocks, flows, feedback loops, delays, the twelve leverage points, and the system traps.
- `lateral-thinking.md`: Edward de Bono. Escaping the dominant pattern through quota, reversal, random stimulation, and suspended judgment.
- `art-of-doing-science-and-engineering.md`: Richard Hamming. Problem selection, back-of-envelope modeling, fundamentals over facts, and how breakthroughs actually happen.

## Systems and complexity (V2)
- `death-and-life-great-american-cities.md`: Jane Jacobs. Organized complexity, generators of diversity, the failure of top-down planning, and reasoning from the real built world.
- `seeing-like-a-state.md`: James C. Scott. Legibility, high-modernism and its catastrophes, metis (local knowledge), and dating a success before praising it.
- `systemantics.md`: John Gall. How systems fail, growing complex systems from simple working ones, goal-displacement, and feedback going lossy at scale.
- `fifth-discipline.md`: Peter Senge. Structure over blame, the system archetypes, the ladder of inference, leverage, and seeing yourself inside the system.

## Creativity and ideation (V2)
- `act-of-creation.md`: Arthur Koestler. Bisociation (forcing two unrelated frames to collide) as the root of the creative act, and incubation.
- `six-thinking-hats.md`: Edward de Bono. Parallel thinking, running the six modes as separate passes, and quarantining criticism.
- `whack-on-the-side-of-the-head.md`: Roger von Oech. The ten mental locks, the second right answer, and the explorer/artist/judge/warrior roles.
- `where-good-ideas-come-from.md`: Steven Johnson. The adjacent possible, the slow hunch, liquid networks, exaptation, and the long zoom.
- `change-by-design.md`: Tim Brown. Design thinking, the diverge-then-converge double diamond, prototyping, and human-centered framing.
- `design-of-everyday-things.md`: Don Norman. Affordances and signifiers, the gulfs of execution and evaluation, solving the correct problem, and blaming the system over the person.
- `designing-your-life.md`: Burnett and Evans. Reframing gravity versus anchor problems, prototyping a life, and generating multiple plans.
- `creative-confidence.md`: Tom and David Kelley. Creativity as a learnable muscle, building self-belief through small wins, and deep empathy for users.
- `creative-habit.md`: Twyla Tharp. Ritualizing the start, scratching for ideas, the spine, and habit and constraint as the engines of output.
- `creative-act-way-of-being.md`: Rick Rubin. Attention to the source, distrusting the self-story, and incorporating the accident.
- `lessons-in-creativity.md`: Ben Rennie. Aiming higher on purpose, and creativity as a practical daily discipline.

## Cognition and analogy (V2)
- `surfaces-and-essences.md`: Hofstadter and Sander. Analogy as the core of cognition, surface versus deep structure, and mapping where an analogy breaks.
- `godel-escher-bach.md`: Douglas Hofstadter. Strange loops, isomorphism, levels of description, and emergence.
- `society-of-mind.md`: Marvin Minsky. The mind as a society of dumb agents, reformulating the problem, and locating where a skill breaks.
- `minds-i.md`: Hofstadter and Dennett. Thought experiments on self, mind, and machine, and keeping your levels straight.

## Uncertainty and risk (V2)
- `antifragile.md`: Nassim Taleb. The fragile-robust-antifragile triad, convexity and optionality, the barbell, via negativa, and iatrogenics.
- `black-swan.md`: Nassim Taleb. Mediocristan versus Extremistan, the Black Swan test, the ludic and narrative fallacies, and relocating from prediction to exposure.
- `fooled-by-randomness.md`: Nassim Taleb. Survivorship and silent evidence, alternative histories, noise versus signal, and weighing magnitude over probability.

## Models and formal thinking (V2)
- `model-thinker.md`: Scott Page. Many-model thinking, models wrong on purpose, REDCAPE, matching distribution to mechanism, and the random-walk null.
- `intro-statistical-learning.md`: James, Witten, Hastie, Tibshirani. Bias-variance, overfitting and out-of-sample error, cross-validation, and regularization.
- `models-of-my-life.md`: Herbert Simon. Bounded rationality, satisficing, aspiration adjustment, and the rational ceiling versus the likely path.
- `art-of-strategy.md`: Dixit and Nalebuff. Backward induction, best response and dominant strategies, Nash equilibrium, the prisoners' dilemma, and credible commitment.
